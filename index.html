How I got here

From the support queue to the product team. Same problem, different seat.

I started in customer experience and operations. Not as a stepping stone, as a genuine education. For four years I was the person users reached when something broke. I built the triage systems, set up the QA frameworks, structured how the team handled every channel from social to Trustpilot. I learned what friction actually costs people, not just in time but in trust.

When I moved into product, I brought all of that with me. My first years were in consumer fintech: Barter, a payments product built for people who needed financial tools to feel like they were made for them, then Flutterwave Mobile, the super app that came after. We crossed 200,000 users. The number was not the point. The point was whether someone in Lagos or Accra could open the app and do what they came to do without hitting a wall.

Over time I moved into infrastructure. The V4 API redesign was about the developer on the other end of our documentation: the one trying to integrate payments at midnight before a deadline. We made that easier. Flutterwave Vouchers answered a question nobody had cleanly solved: how does a global brand accept M-Pesa, Opay, or MTN MoMo without rebuilding their entire payment setup? We figured it out.

Now I am also building Bucklid, a marketplace for African creators and brands, backed by escrow and structured around goals. The problems I care about are not limited to fintech. They show up anywhere trust and money need to move together without a safety net.
